Research Work

About Research

Computational Materials Engineering and Process Modelling is a fast growing area in the field of metallurgical engineering. Our group is dedicated to explore and understand basic metallurgical phenomena using simulation and modelling techniques.

Atomistic Modelling of deformation of Materials

Atomistic simulation of nanoscale processes

Design and development of application specific new functional materials based on the outcome of simulation studies at atomic level.
This includes:

  1. Molecular Dynamics based study of deformation behavior of material at nanoscale.
  2. Density Functional based study for designing application specific materials.

Steel Microstructure Modelling

Prediction and characterization of the evolution of microstructure at the mesoscale level.
This includes:

  1. Digitization of Microstructures
  2. Phase Transformation
  3. Solidification
  4. Interface problems

Our group is actively working on the micro structure modelling of steel. Simulations for heating of dual phase steels has been performed.

Metallurgical process modelling

Statistical and Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) modelling of metallurgical processes to optimize the process parameters.
This includes:

  1. Computer Modelling of Welding Process
  2. Continuous casting process of steel
  3. Argon Oxygen Decarburization (AOD) process.
